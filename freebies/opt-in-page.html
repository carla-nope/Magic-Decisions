Why This Helps

Small Choices Can Feel Surprisingly Big

You probably spent 15 minutes choosing a restaurant last week. Or agonizing over which streaming show to start. Or debating whether to use the self-checkout or the regular checkout. Here's the uncomfortable truth: none of that time was worth it.

The decision didn't change your life. It just stole your attention.

This guide was created for parents who feel like every small decision — what to make for dinner, who picks the movie, which park to visit — turns into a 30-minute negotiation. It's a friendly starting point, not a clinical tool or a therapy resource.

"The goal isn't to stop making decisions. The goal is to stop making some decisions mean more than they do."
