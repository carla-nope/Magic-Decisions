## 100 Tiny Decisions You Don't Need to Overthink (Just Pick and Move On)

Here's a secret: most of the decisions that drain your mental energy don't actually matter. Flip a coin. Spin a wheel. Move on with your day. This list proves it.

You probably spent 15 minutes choosing a restaurant last week. Or agonizing over which streaming service show to start. Or debating whether to use the self-checkout or the regular checkout. Here's the uncomfortable truth: none of that time was worth it. The decision didn't change your life. It just stole your attention.

This list exists to remind you: some decisions are genuinely tiny. They deserve seconds of thought, not minutes. They deserve a coin flip, not a committee meeting. And when you learn to recognize these tiny decisions and let them go, your brain gets freed up for the things that actually matter.

## The Takeaway

This list isn't about being reckless. It's about recognizing that your attention is finite. Every minute you spend debating cereal brands is a minute not spent on things that genuinely matter to you and your family.
> "The goal isn't to stop making decisions. The goal is to stop making some decisions mean more than they do."

Next time you catch yourself spiraling over something small, come back to this list. Ask: is this a decision I should invest brain power in, or a decision I should make in seconds and move on? The answer will often surprise you with how little these tiny choices actually matter.
