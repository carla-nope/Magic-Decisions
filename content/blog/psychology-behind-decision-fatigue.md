# The Psychology Behind Decision Fatigue

Every day, we make countless decisions—from what to wear in the morning to what to have for dinner. While these choices may seem trivial, research shows they can accumulate and deplete our mental energy throughout the day.

## What Is Decision Fatigue?

Decision fatigue is the deteriorating quality of decisions made after a long session of decision-making. Just like a muscle, our capacity for making good choices gets tired with use.

Psychologist Roy Baumeister famously demonstrated this phenomenon in a study where people who resisted tempting sweets were later more likely to give up on difficult puzzles. The same principle applies to everyday choices.

## The Hidden Cost of Micro-Decisions

Consider how many small decisions you make before noon:

- What to wear
- What to eat for breakfast
- Which route to take to work
- What to check first in your email

Each decision, even tiny ones, draws from the same finite pool of mental energy. By the time you reach an important choice—like whether to take that job offer or invest in that opportunity—your decision-making capacity may already be depleted.

**3. They Make Decisions Fair**
In group settings, tools ensure no one feels pressured to always accommodate others. The tool decides, so there's no lingering resentment.
